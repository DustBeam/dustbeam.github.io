FRC Trading Card Game Rules

Card Types

  • Common: Buff Uncommon cards. Buffs return to the player’s hand when the affected card is destroyed.
  • Uncommon: Can attack on your turn and defend on the opponent’s turn.
  • Rare: Provide special effects or buffs. Must be played face down first, then flipped to activate.
  • Unique: Can act as any of the above but must also be played face down first, then flipped.

Setup

  1. (Optional) Gather two types of small counters (e.g., nuts and bolts) to track attack and health.
  2. Each player selects 10 cards to use for the entire game. This is their hand and deck.
  3. Each player places 1 Uncommon card face down on the field.
  4. Once both players have placed their card, they flip it over simultaneously.
  5. Determine which player goes first.

Gameplay

  • Each turn, a player gets 2 actions.
  • Actions include:
    • Flipping a Rare or Unique card.
    • Attacking with a card.
    • Playing a card.
    • Using a buff card on another card.
    • Discarding a card from the field.
  • Attacking:
    • Choose an opponent’s card with health to attack.
    • Subtract the attacker's attack value from the defender’s health.
    • If the defender’s health reaches 0, it is discarded.
    • If the opponent has no cards with health, you can attack them directly.
    • Excess damage does not carry over to the player.
  • Buff Mechanics:
    • Buffs from Common and Rare cards remain until the affected card is destroyed.
    • When the buffed card is destroyed, the buff card returns to the player’s hand.
  • Face-Down Rare & Unique Cards:
    • There is no limit to how many can be placed face down.
    • They must be flipped to activate their effects.
  • Running Out of Cards:
    • If a player runs out of cards in hand, they can still take actions.
    • The discard pile is not recycled.

Winning & Losing

  • Each player starts with 20 health.
  • If a player's health reaches 0, they lose.

Other Edge Cases

  • As long as both players agree, handle anything not mentioned here however you want.

But most importantly...

  • Don't forget to have fun!
